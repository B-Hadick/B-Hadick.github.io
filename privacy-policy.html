Privacy Policy for Eat First

Effective Date: 05-01-2026

Eat First is a food expiration tracking app designed to help users track pantry, fridge, and freezer items, scan product barcodes, manage shopping lists, and receive expiration reminders.

Information Users Enter

Eat First may store food item names, quantities, storage locations, expiration dates, notes, shopping list items, alert preferences, and app settings entered by the user.

This information is stored on the user’s device unless a future version clearly adds optional cloud sync.

Camera and Barcode Scanning

Eat First may request access to the device camera so users can scan product barcodes.

Camera access is used only for barcode scanning. Eat First does not store camera photos or videos.

When a barcode is scanned, the app may use the barcode number to look up product information, such as product name or related product details, if that feature is enabled in the app.

Barcode scan results and auto-filled product information may be saved locally as part of the user’s food item list.

Expiration Dates

Eat First may allow users to enter expiration dates manually. If the app suggests or auto-fills expiration-related information, users should verify the information before relying on it.

Expiration reminders are provided for convenience and should not be treated as food-safety advice.

Purchases and Subscriptions

Eat First may offer optional paid subscriptions through Apple App Store In-App Purchase or Google Play Billing.

Subscription status and purchase validation may be handled by RevenueCat, Apple, and Google Play as needed to process purchases, restore purchases, and manage premium access.

Local Storage

Eat First uses local device storage to save food tracking data, shopping list data, barcode scan results, product information, app settings, expiration alert preferences, and premium feature preferences.

Data Sharing

Eat First does not sell user data.

Eat First may share limited purchase-related information with RevenueCat, Apple, or Google only as needed to process subscriptions, validate purchases, restore purchases, and manage premium access.

If barcode lookup uses a third-party product database or API, barcode numbers may be sent to that service to retrieve product information. Eat First does not send camera photos or videos for barcode lookup.

Data Deletion

Users can delete food items, shopping list items, and app data inside the app. Users may also uninstall the app to remove locally stored app data from their device.

Children’s Privacy

Eat First is not directed toward children under 13.
