Privacy Policy for Eat First

Effective Date: 05-01-2026

Eat First is a food expiration tracking app designed to help users track pantry, fridge, and freezer items, scan product barcodes, manage shopping lists, and receive expiration reminders.

Information Users Enter

Eat First may store food item names, quantities, storage locations, expiration dates, notes, shopping list items, alert preferences, and app settings entered by the user.

This information is stored locally on the user’s device unless a future version clearly adds optional cloud sync.

Camera and Barcode Scanning

Eat First may request access to the device camera so users can scan product barcodes.

Camera access is used only for barcode scanning. Barcode detection happens on the device using the app’s camera scanning feature.

Eat First does not store camera photos, video, or camera frames.

When a barcode is scanned, Eat First may send the barcode number to Open Food Facts to look up product information, such as the product name or related product details.

The barcode number is sent to Open Food Facts using a product lookup request. Eat First does not store the barcode number after the lookup is complete.

If the user chooses to save the scanned item, any auto-filled product information saved by the user may be stored locally on the user’s device as part of their food tracking list.

Expiration Dates and Reminders

Eat First allows users to enter expiration dates manually and may provide reminders based on those dates.

If the app suggests or auto-fills expiration-related information, users should verify the information before relying on it.

Expiration reminders are provided for convenience and should not be treated as food-safety advice. Users are responsible for checking food condition, packaging, storage instructions, and safety before consuming any item.

Purchases and Subscriptions

Eat First may offer optional paid subscriptions through Apple App Store In-App Purchase or Google Play Billing.

Subscription status and purchase validation may be handled by RevenueCat, Apple, and Google Play as needed to process purchases, restore purchases, and manage premium access.

Local Storage

Eat First uses local device storage to save food tracking data, shopping list data, saved product information, app settings, expiration alert preferences, and premium feature preferences.

Scanned barcode numbers are not stored after product lookup unless a future version clearly adds that feature.

Data Sharing

Eat First does not sell user data.

Eat First may send scanned barcode numbers to Open Food Facts only for product lookup. Camera photos, video, and camera frames are not sent to Open Food Facts.

Eat First may share limited purchase-related information with RevenueCat, Apple, or Google only as needed to process subscriptions, validate purchases, restore purchases, and manage premium access.

Third-Party Services

Eat First may use the following third-party services:

  • Open Food Facts: used to look up product information from scanned barcode numbers.
  • RevenueCat: used to help manage subscription status, purchase validation, and premium access.
  • Apple App Store and Google Play: used to process in-app purchases and subscriptions.

Data Deletion

Users can delete food items, shopping list items, and app data inside the app. Users may also uninstall the app to remove locally stored app data from their device.

Children’s Privacy

Eat First is not directed toward children under 13.

Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date.
